Temporal Distribution of Complaints

The most significant monthly/daily patterns that can be identified:

  • Heating Patterns: Complaints have a seasonal pattern with maximum volume between October to April. In 2016, there seems to be a longer and colder winter than the preceding year increasing the number of calls in November-December-May.
  • Noise Patterns: Complaints peaked between April to October and Friday to Sunday. There is consistency of the monthly and daily patterns with night life and outdoor activities, considering warmer months and weekends. Taking into account that the most popular description of Noise complaints is Loud Music/Party, the above notion is strengthened.
  • Water System Patterns: The number of calls increases in July and August through weekdays, during summer when people are more likely to stay in the city due to work activities, showing that NYC suffers from a poor water supply in the summer.
  • Street Condition: Peak period is between February to June and Monday to Thursday. These are days and months were business activities are higher resulting in a higher usage of the road network, vehicles or pedestrians.
