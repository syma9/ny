Overall, there appears to be a negative correlation between median income and the total number of the top 10 complaints type. This is contradicts with the findings of Warland et.al (1975) whereby income was found to be positively correlated to complaining behaviour. However, individual regression of each complaint type against median income yields varying results. It was observed that median income varies positively with complaints regarding the 'external environment'; but varies negatively with complaints regarding the 'internal environment'. It is probable that more affluent individuals may live in housing with asset managers who looks after the 'internal environment', on the other hand, the less wealthy individuals may not have this luxury and could only turn to 311 as a resolution avenue for their complaints.
